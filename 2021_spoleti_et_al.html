A reduced self-consistent set of files needed to reproduce Fig.5A, 5B of the paper is available on ModelDB.

The simulations will reproduce the experimental traces of CA1 ventral hippocampal neurons from 3 months-old (Fig.5A) and 8 months-old (Fig.5B) mice, in wild type (WT) and Tg2576 (TG) conditions.

The model indicates the depolarization block and the derailment of specific conductances (INa; KDR; KCa) as possible ionic mechanisms underlying the altered excitability at early phase of Alzheimer disease.

HOWTO: Press buttons in the "3 months-old mice" panel to set the parameters used to obtain the traces in Fig.5A and then click "Run":
  • The"WT 3m 0.25nA" and "WT 3m 0.5nA" buttons will allow to reproduce the 3-months WT cell's trace at 0.25nA and 0.5nA respectively
  • The "TG 3m 0.25nA" and "TG 3m 0.5nA" buttons will allow to reproduce the 3-months TG cell's trace at 0.25nA and 0.5nA respectively
Press buttons in the "8 months-old mice" panel to set the parameters used to obtain the traces in Fig.5B:
  • The "WT 8m 0.2nA" and 'WT 8m 0.25nA" buttons will allow to reproduce the 8-months WT cell's trace at 0.2nA and 0.25nA respectively
  • The "TG 8m 0.2nA" and 'TG 8m 0.25nA" buttons will allow to reproduce the 8-months TG cell's trace at 0.2nA and 0.25nA respectively
Additionally, you can set the kcaallnoax and the kcaallnoax conductances to explore the model behavior.

NOTE: the online simulator might run with a NEURON version different than the one used for the simulations reported in the paper. Hence, you might see minor differences in the simulated activity (e.g., number of spikes).
